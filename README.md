# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI
## 📌 About the Project
This project is an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard developed in Power BI using an employee dataset. The main purpose of building this dashboard was to practice data modeling, DAX calculations, and dashboard design while solving common HR reporting requirements.

The dashboard provides a complete overview of employee information, including attrition, department performance, salary distribution, job satisfaction, age groups, gender distribution, and work experience. All visuals are connected with a department slicer, allowing users to explore the data from different perspectives.
## 🎯 Project Goal

The goal of this project was to convert raw HR data into a meaningful dashboard that can help answer questions such as:

- Which departments have the highest employee attrition?
- How does attrition vary across salary ranges?
- Which age groups make up most of the workforce?
- What is the relationship between job satisfaction and employee attrition?
- How is the workforce distributed across departments?
## 📊 Dashboard KPIs

The dashboard tracks the following key HR metrics:

- Total Employees
- Active Employees
- Employee Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate (%)
- Average Employee Age
- Average Years of Experience

These KPIs provide a quick summary of the organization's workforce.

## 📈 Dashboard Pages & Analysis

### Employee Summary
Displays overall workforce statistics using KPI cards for quick business reporting.

### Attrition by Department
Compares employee attrition across different departments to identify where turnover is highest.

### Attrition by Salary Slab
Shows how employee exits are distributed across different salary ranges.

### Job Role vs Job Satisfaction
A matrix visual that compares employee attrition against job roles and satisfaction ratings.

### Age Group Distribution
Breaks employees into different age groups to understand workforce demographics.

### Attrition by Gender
Shows the distribution of attrition between male and female employees.

### Experience Trend
Analyzes employee attrition based on total years of experience.

### Department-wise Employee Count
Displays the number of employees working in each department.

## 📌 Key Insights
Some observations from the dashboard include:
- Employee attrition is not evenly distributed across departments.
- Most employees belong to the 26–45 age group.
- Salary level appears to influence attrition patterns.
- Job satisfaction varies across different job roles.
- The department slicer makes it easy to compare workforce metrics across business units.
